# How small teams ship faster than large ones

**Source:** https://example.com/small-teams-ship-faster
**Author:** J. Doe
**Captured:** 2026-04-22

A short clipped argument that small teams ship faster because they
have fewer coordination edges. The author cites a 2024 internal
study at a large tech company showing that teams of 4-6 produced
twice the merged-PR rate per engineer of teams of 12+.

The clipping includes a diagram of the coordination-edge growth
curve:

![[example-diagram.png]]

The author concedes one counter-case: when the work is genuinely
parallelisable across many independent surfaces (e.g. a localisation
sweep), large teams win. But for product work where everyone touches
the same code path, the curve dominates.
